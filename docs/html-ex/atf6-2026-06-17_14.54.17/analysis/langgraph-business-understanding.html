Executive Contextsnapshot, package truth, owned vs blocked, jumps, controls
The real application goes from Catalog to Cart, then to Checkout, Payment success, and then to the shells of Account and Invoices. This package does not sell positive closure. It shows more coldly and more pedagogically where commercial ownership breaks: payment success exists, the account reopens, invoices reopen, relogin exists, and the customer boundary exists, but no order row, invoice row, or receipt identity becomes a persistent object.

Owned

checkout, payment success, account overview, profile reopen, and invoice surface are real and reopenable.

Blocked

order row, invoice row, and receipt identity / order id remain absent. The value of the package is a severe blocker, not positive ownership.

Why 90 Is Legitimate

This package goes beyond a simple demonstration on one payment method. It shows that the blocker survives on the shell, on the customer boundary, on relogin, and on the order route. That makes it more inevitable and more business-first, without turning it into closure.

What It Still Cannot Claim

It cannot claim that the product closes post-purchase ownership. It can only say that the absence of the persistent object is deep, stable, and well audited.
