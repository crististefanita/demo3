Score-card rule MUST do this Avoid this failure

Score precisionUse precise decimal scores and keep one consistent local style across the four cards, normally one or two decimals such as 9.7 or 9.71.Integer-only flattening or mixed precision that feels arbitrary.
Card explanationKeep each explanation short and concrete: why the score is here, what still blocks a higher score.Soft adjectives with no blocking reason.

Comparison restraintIf brief comparison language is truly needed, keep it secondary, specific, and subordinate to the local verdict.Benchmark chatter that overwhelms the opening.

Final-page truth MUST state clearly
Product and proofWhat the product appears to be and what the round truly proved.
Code delta splitWhat changed under src/test and what changed under src/main.
Run-quality and output accountingWhich kept runs cleared RUN_EXPECTED_DELTA_QUALITY, what defended branch coverage each one added, how much of that scope was materially turned into tests, and why the round did or did not reach ROUND_TARGET_TEST_COUNT.
Deepest chainThe deepest defended chain in one explicit arrow-like sequence when that matters to understanding.
Retained-close honestyWhether broader retained-close truly absorbed the proof-origin truth or only reran around it.
Gap pictureThe strongest current benchmark gap and strongest current frontier gap.
Gap reductionWhether the round materially reduced either one.
Open next moveWhat still remains open and what the next honest move is.
Replacement-grade judgmentIf the report is being positioned as a new strong local benchmark or html-ex candidate, state what older local package it displaced, what it still does not beat, and why.
Atlas-grade audit sectionsWhen the round is broad enough, include the atlas-grade sections explicitly: Automation Coverage Overlay, Test Mapping, Iteration Delta View, Accessible Hyperlink Exploration Ledger, Flow To Support To Proof Mapping, Coverage Vocabulary, Route-Proof Versus Flow-Proof Delta, Current Baseline Decision, Disconfirmed Frontier Notes, Cross-Run Comparison, Benchmark Comparison, Checkpoint Self-Evaluation, Iteration History And Score Evolution, Self-Perfection Audit Trail, and Render Verification Status when they are genuinely earned by the round.
6.2 Optional reporting variants when requested OPTIONAL
Variant rule MUST keep true
Primary outputKeep the canonical HTML as the primary output when this standard is invoked directly.
Narrow variantsSupport narrower requested variants only when asked, such as a brief summary, score-only view, milestone view, history index, mapping-first page, or proof-link digest.
Meaning consistencyPreserve the same meaning across variants: product truth, trust basis, coverage state, retained evidence anchors, and next move stay consistent.
Ownership boundaryTreat alternate formats as reporting variants only. Retention, reference-memory copying, exemplar decisions, and upstream publication language stay owned by ORCHESTRATION_PATH and QUALITY_LOOP_PATH.
6.3 What the final page must avoid CORE
Final-page anti-pattern Why it must be avoided
Winner-centric top wording when evolution-first is clearerIt distorts what the round actually learned and defended.
Low-value artifacts displacing decisive linksIt makes trust reconstruction slower and noisier.
Coverage claims exceeding observed product behaviorIt turns the report into overclaim rather than defended understanding.
Report polish hiding package thinness, evidence mismatch, or weak retained confidenceIt lets aesthetics mask an audit defect.
Language implying promotion, exemplar status, or upstream integration without owning contractIt crosses ownership boundaries and inflates status dishonestly.
